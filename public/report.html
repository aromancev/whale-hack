Resolved and Recovered Case Rates

The Netherlands and United States show relatively high reported recovery or resolution rates. In countries with larger free-roaming animal populations and less mature formal pet-control systems, expected recovery is substantially lower.

Disclaimer: “Resolved” or “recovered” cases do not necessarily mean every pet was physically found and returned home. Depending on the source and reporting system, a case may also be closed because the animal was confirmed found, the owner was identified, the report was withdrawn, or the outcome was otherwise clarified.

India
High-risk recovery environment

Mars’ State of Pet Homelessness research reports a very large homeless companion-animal population in India, indicating weaker reunification conditions for lost pets.

Mexico
High-risk recovery environment

Mars’ Mexico market report highlights large street and shelter populations, making lost-pet recovery more difficult than in highly registered pet systems.

Why it matters

Millions of pets go missing each year. In markets with larger free-roaming animal populations, better reporting, identification, and matching tools can have an even larger impact.
